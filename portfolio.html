Phase one

We diverged exploring 3 areas of water safety: Public Pools, Domestic Pools, Beaches. Based on our research we proceed to focus on beach safety. However we feel that much of our work into beach safety would be applicable to the other scenarios.

Phase two

What can we do to help improve safety across Australia's beaches.

The heart and sole of beach safety on our beaches are the Surf Life saving volunteers and the Surf Lifeguard employees. Together they are responsible for patrolling sand and seas across the nation and building a strong culture of water safety through education and community outreach.

To tackle this problem we need to understand what tools these stakeholders wanted. We conducted in depth interviews with a number of Surf life savers and guards to help us understand their needs.

Pain points

  • It can be easy to miss someone when the water is choppy and or busy.
  • It is hard to tell if someone is actually in distress.
  • Giving a clear location of a person in distress can be difficult.
  • Prevention is best, understanding the conditions to predict hazards.

Desires

  • Tools that are very easy to use.
  • Needs to work in a rough beach environment.
  • Nothing that distracts from our current duties.
  • Any changes to our current procedure would have to be very robust.
