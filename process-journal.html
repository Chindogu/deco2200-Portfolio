Week Two

Intelligent transportation systems and the robot city The work of Martin and Stewart really opened my eyes to the scope of an Autonomous world and forced me to ask myself about it’s implications morally and economically. I am fascinated with the concept of uncertainty in an AI system and how you would be able to communicate the systems state or ‘feeling’ to a user. Further to this I think that there would be some cool applications for AI augmented interfaces making use of this sensor data. The limitations of flying drones outlined in the lecture helped inform our choice to not use a prop powered device for our final concept.

Week Three

User Research

Hendrik Muller's work was really inspiring I loved his presentation style and the way he carried us through projects he had done while explaining the steps. The fact that they where examples I had used and could relate to made it all the better. The inclusion of real world limitations to his decision making process was really refreshing, I find the whole blue sky no limits thinking a little tiresome. His lecture slides were really helpful when deciding on what methods to use in assessment 1&2.

Interviewing Surf Life Savers

We developed some research objectives and questions for assessment one.

Research Objectives:

  • Identify common processes of rescue operations in pools and beaches.
  • Identify common problems with rescue operations in pools and beaches.

Interview Questions:

  • How long have you been a Surf Life Guard?
  • Have you ever been involved in a rescue?
  • What is the most common issue for surf life savers on patrol?
  • What's the most common rescue scenario on patrol?
  • Who are the different stakeholders involved in that rescue? From top to bottom
  • Can you elaborate on what the average rescue process for [the common rescue scenario]?

Week Four

Connected Devices and IOT

To put a chip or not to put a chip. We have witnessed the rise of IOT devices over that last few years for better or for worse this devices are now commonplace in many people's lives. However they are plagued with problems that often stop them from delivering on the lifestyle they promised. In addition to network latency and sensor tech that just isn’t quite there yet they also pose many security concerns for their users. They hold a great deal of promise for the future however I feel we should be cautious to adopt them to hastaly.

goo.gl/PQhQj9

We did explore how these concepts could be applied to our area of study and this inspired some concepts like the IOT Flag for surf life saving and wearable devices to aid in rescue. The ideas of personification we explored in the lecture came back in our final concept image. We chose to implement them quite literally and try to make our blimp as friendly as possible to help people to feel more comfortable with it’s presence.

Week Five

Design Communication

I really loved Stephanie Grace’s work and presentation, her reasoning behind the importance of effective communication was fantastic. The way she layed out the order and application for different methods of communication was excellent. This really crystalised for me how these all fit together and could be used as tools in a practical way.

Paper prototypes made to test concepts

The Tutorial exercise helped me to appreciate the usefulness of paper prototyping for UI design. I had previously been skeptical of its use when there where so many digital drawing tools available. However I found that not only was it fast but that working in 1:1 helped me to appreciate the scale and position of the elements I was creating.

Paper Prototyping of Mobile UI
Design Patterns for UI

The explanations of how each term stacked with the next from Principles - Best Practice - Patterns - Components. This really worked for me and helped to clear some of the confusion I had around these terms. Understanding the steps involved in developing a complex UI was really eye opening and I feel this process is applicable to any system or product not just Digital UI’s.

Managing and Evaluating low fidelity prototypes

I’m still skeptical of the efficiency of this method, I understand the benefit of hand sketching for a first pass on UI concepts. However I feel as though there would be some reasonable time savings to be had by generating these digitally and printing them instead of hand drawing each screen. I could be that I am not very fast at sketching but still I found I spend a lot of time redrawing the same basic screens with minor variations and that took a lot of time. I’m also found it very hard to engage with the interface to give meaningful feedback as it was very busy in its hand sketched state. Even basic things like text size and clarity was very difficult with hand drawing. Not to discount the benefits of being able to test complex interactions without programming and to quickly change how those interactions behave on the fly. I do see it’s merits but I’m not sure it’s time efficient.

Wireframing our app

Our group started developing wireframes for assessment 2 we used many of the strategies covered in week 5 & 6. Assessing the usability of these within our group and made rapid iteration based on this feedback to help us hone in our concept.

Framer

The brief tutorial in framer was a really nice way to get us started however having now build a fairly complex app with the language I don’t feel it really prepared us. I would have benefited from a deeper explanation of the languages structure and how it deals with different screens or pages. However the kick start was definitely appreciated. I really appreciate that we are introduced to a wide variety of tools and given the freedom to work with whatever takes our interest. While I regret that I haven't taken the opportunity to experiment with all of them the exposure to them has broadened my scope.

Week Nine

Testing with real users
Usability Evaluation

It’s one thing to design something with the user in mind, but unless we test our design with our users - we won’t know if we’ve been successful.

It was very helpful to understand where in the design process the usability evaluation is used. Understanding the correct approach to doing these evaluations was an integral part of our users testing.
